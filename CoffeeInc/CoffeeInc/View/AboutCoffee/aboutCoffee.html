More about our Coffee Beans...

Catuaí coffee Robusta coffee
Created by the Agronomic Institute of Campinas, the Catuaí is a species developed completely in Brazilian territory. Currently, 45% of coffee crops produce this species. It is one of the most produced grains in the world and this is because it is a type that adapts to different environments, due to its resistance.
In 1972, it was officially released to consumers in the market. The variety is the result of a cross between Mundo Novo and Caturra beans. When tasting a drink made with Robusta beans, you will notice a smooth texture, low acidity and a heavy body due to the bitter notes.
There are Red and Yellow variations, the first one being more full-bodied and with more accentuated bitterness, and the second one being softer and more delicate on the palate. Specialty coffee from the Robusta line and its varieties are highly coveted and, when cultivated with care, produce coffees that are rich in quality.
Planting Catuaí is simple. The method was developed to facilitate cultivation for growers, with shorter and more resistant plants. Plantations located above 1,000 meters generate higher quality drinks.
The name of the coffee, in fact, is a tribute to the Tupi-Guarani Indians, and means “very good”.
